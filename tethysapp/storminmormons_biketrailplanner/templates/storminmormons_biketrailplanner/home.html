Bike Trail Planner

Developed by Team Stormin Mormons

This app was developed at Brigham Young University, as part of a requirement for Geospatial Software Development (CE En 514), taught by professor Daniel P. Ames.

The purpose of this app is two-fold. 1) To provide bicycle trail planners with a feasibility analysis for locating a potential route; and 2) to pass this class.

To operate or execute this application, users will:

    help determine the location for the route by choosing the beginning and ending trail location.
    tailor the route to pass close to or avoid certain features.
    have the option to select the average grade for the trail.

After these inputs have been received, users will then click the "Refresh" button. The total distance, estimated time of travel, and an elevation profile plot of the route will be generated.

If users are unsatisfied with the results, or if users desire to change certain inputs, simply modify any parameter and re-click "Refresh". A new set of results will automatically generate.

To navigate to this feature and application, click the "Main Map Page" on the left-hand side of the window, or simply click here.
